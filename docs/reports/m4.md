# M4 report — Channels & the Shadow

> Reconstructed from the build record (the file convention began at the next
> milestone, M5). M4 is commit `63eb827`.

Scope: PRD §13 M4 — channel routes and rail (all five kinds with derived
membership), the composer with optimistic send, the 5s visibility-aware poll,
the deterministic Shadow, and the draft request/approve/discard flow.

## What shipped

- `ChannelThread` carries every channel kind: day-grouped messages, the third
  and final sanctioned optimistic send, and a 5-second poll that pauses while the
  document is hidden, backs its cursor off ten seconds against out-of-order
  commits, and dedupes by id. `sendMessage` and `fetchMessagesAfter` derive
  rights from `lib/channels.ts` — posting is membership; clients post only in
  their own account thread; DMs never admit clients or parked users (pinned by
  tests).
- The deterministic Shadow goes interactive: "Draft update" locks the channel
  row before counting pending drafts (the §9 aggregate rule), the loader reads
  only the §7.3 facts from human messages, approval stamps the approver and bumps
  the message to its approval moment so every window converges within a cycle,
  attributed "Shadow · approved by …" in muted, badge-free text; discard deletes,
  and stale panels yield cleanly when the other window decided first.
- `startDm` finds-or-creates the deterministic employee-pair channel from the
  profile's Message affordance; DMs nest under a DIRECT rail eyebrow. Completed
  jobs' channels read archived and refuse new drafts server-side. The portal
  thread is live for clients with the review-call prefill, no hub links, and tier
  badges suppressed as internal vocabulary.
- Rulings folded into PRD.md: the aggregate-lock extension, auto-enroll, badge
  scope, the `startDm` path, the §7.3 workers/moderators/admins alignment, and
  the §4 account-thread membership row.
